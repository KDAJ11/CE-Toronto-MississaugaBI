03 — What to Post
CONTENT PILLARS
Seven types of content. Post ideas for each.
Sunday Service
2–3x per week
  • "This Sunday" promo with time + topic
  • 30-sec worship clip or sermon moment
  • Photo carousel: people, worship, Word
  • Monday recap with key quote from message
Quotes & Devotionals
3–4x per week
  • Scripture of the day — clean, editorial layout
  • Pastor quote on cinematic still from Sunday
  • Midweek encouragement: relatable caption + visual
  • Rhapsody of Realities daily confession
Pastor & Leadership
1–2x per week
  • Portrait photo with sermon quote overlay
  • 60-second sermon clip for Reels/TikTok
  • Leadership spotlight: who's behind the scenes
  • Q&A story series with the Pastor
Events & The Hub
Event-driven
  • Football tournament — bold, high-energy design
  • Healing Streams Live — countdown + streaming link
  • The Hub youth events: socials, sports, dev nights
  • Post-event photo dump carousel
Behind the Scenes
1–2x per week
  • Media team setting up before service
  • Volunteers in action: ushers, kids church
  • Before/after: empty room to full worship
  • "Day in the life" Reel following a team member
Testimonies
1x per week
  • Member story: photo + written testimony carousel
  • Video testimony: 60–90 sec sit-down interview
  • "God did this" — text-on-screen Reel with music
  • Healing Streams testimony spotlight
Reels & Short-Form
3–5x per week
  • Worship clip with lyrics overlay — cinematic
  • Sermon punchline moment — 15-sec hook
  • Trending audio with church twist
  • POV: walking into CE Toronto for the first time

05 — Voice & Tone
HOW WE SPEAK
Casual, warm, real. Like texting a friend who has something powerful to say.
Sunday Caption
"That moment when worship hits different and you forget you had a rough week. See you Sunday. 10 AM. Don't be late."
Casual, warm. Not a corporate announcement — a friend texting you.
Event Promo
"The Hub Football Tournament is BACK. Grab your squad, lace up, and come ready to compete. Bragging rights are on the line."
Energetic, direct. Short sentences. Action-oriented.
Devotional
"Some days faith feels easy. Some days it feels like you're carrying the weight of everything. Either way — He's carrying you."
Honest, gentle, conversational. Meets people where they are.
Behind the Scenes
"POV: the media team at 7 AM making sure everything sounds right so you can have the best Sunday. These are the real MVPs."
Fun, appreciative, a little playful.
We say
  • → "Come as you are"
  • → "You belong here"
  • → "See you Sunday"
  • → "Let's go" / "Let's build"
  • → First person: "we" not "the church"
  • → Short, punchy, direct
We don't say
  • → "We cordially invite you to..."
  • → "Greetings beloved brethren"
  • → Guilt-based messaging
  • → "You need to be in church"
  • → Walls of text with no personality
  • → Corporate / formal language

06 — Photo & Video
WHAT TO SHOOT
Six shot types for the media team. Prioritize real moments over posed photos.
The Walk-In
People arriving. Handshakes at the door, families walking in. Says "you're welcome here."
→ Shoot from inside looking toward entrance
Worship Moments
Hands raised, band in full flow. Wide for atmosphere, tight for emotion.
→ Slow-mo clips are gold for Reels
The In-Between
Coffee, kids running, post-service conversations. Candid moments that humanize the brand.
→ Don't pose people — catch real moments
The Setup
Media team running cables. Sound check. The "before" to Sunday's "during."
→ Time-lapse from empty room to full service
The Huddle
Small groups praying. Youth gathering. Teams planning. Community at its most real.
→ Over-the-shoulder angles feel immersive
The Stage
Wide shot during service. Pastor mid-message. The cinematic, premium shot.
→ Tripod + long lens for clean compression
07 — Content Ideas
WHAT TO CREATE
A bank of ready-to-use content ideas. Pick what fits the week, mix and match, and make them your own.
Daily Rhapsody Quote
Post a quote from Rhapsody of Realities to Stories every day. Clean typography on a branded background. Consistent, simple, and it keeps the page active daily.
→ Best for: Stories (daily)
→ Use the quote template with Crimson Pro italic
Church Outfit of the Day
Feature someone's Sunday fit — photo in the lobby or outside the building. Fun, lighthearted, and it shows real people. Ask permission first.
→ Best for: Stories, Reels, Feed carousel
→ Use a "CE OOTD" text overlay in Canva
Department Spotlight
Sit down with someone from sound, media, ushers, kids church — ask what they do, why they serve, what they love about it. 60–90 sec video.
→ Best for: Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts
→ Use "Meet the Team" as a recurring series name
First Time at CE
Catch first-time visitors after service — ask how they heard about CE, what they thought, how they felt. Authentic reactions are the best marketing.
→ Best for: Reels, TikTok
→ "POV: your first Sunday at CE Toronto" format
Song of the Week
Worship team picks a song — post a 30-sec clip of them playing it, or a graphic with the song name and a Spotify/YouTube link. Builds the worship culture online.
→ Best for: Stories, Feed post, Reels
→ Tag the original artist for extra reach
Throwback Moments
Dig into the archives — old event photos, past Night of Bliss, early days of CE Toronto. Nostalgia connects people and shows how far the church has come.
→ Best for: Feed carousel, Stories
→ Use "#ThrowbackThursday" or "#CETBT"
Children's Church Highlights
Fun moments from kids church — crafts, games, worship, the kids being kids. Parents love seeing this, and it shows families there's something for everyone.
→ Best for: Stories, Feed post
→ Always get parent permission before posting kids
Why I Serve
Quick video — a volunteer shares why they serve and what it means to them. Inspires others to get involved. Keep it under 60 seconds, real and unscripted.
→ Best for: Reels, TikTok, Feed
→ Recurring series — one volunteer per week
Guess the Scripture
Post a scripture with a missing word or reference — let people guess in the comments or via Stories poll. Drives engagement and gets people in the Word.
→ Best for: Stories (quiz sticker), Feed
→ Reveal the answer in the next Story slide
Member Shoutout
Birthday celebrations, milestones, achievements — shout out members on Stories. Makes people feel seen and valued. They'll repost it every time.
→ Best for: Stories
→ Create a reusable birthday template in Canva
Tag Someone
Post a relatable quote or encouragement and add "Tag someone who needs to hear this." Simple, effective, and it spreads your content to new people organically.
→ Best for: Feed post, Stories
→ Pair with devotional or sermon quote
Your Idea Here
This list is a starting point. The best content ideas will come from the team — something funny that happened at service, a trending sound that fits, a moment someone captured on their phone. Stay creative.
